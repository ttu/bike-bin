## Design Context

### Users

Bike Bin is built for **knowledgeable, hands-on cyclists** — three overlapping personas:

- **Enthusiast hobbyists** — multiple bikes, home garage, ongoing builds, meticulous about cataloging parts and tools.
- **Discipline specialists** (MTB / gravel / road) — performance-minded, fluent in component vocabulary (groupset tiers, hub standards, tire casings), care about pedigree and condition.
- **Community / club / co-op members** — ride and wrench together; the app's secondary social layer (groups, lending, ratings, messaging) is real to them, not bolted on.

**Primary job-to-be-done:** _"Know what I own, in what condition, and where it lives in my workflow."_
Listing for borrow / donation / sale, nearby search, and group sharing build on top of that — they are not the front door.

**Context of use:** in the garage on a phone with one hand free; on a laptop while planning a build; outdoors in sunlight quickly checking a part spec; late at night reorganizing the parts bin after a ride.

### Brand Personality

**Workshop-warm, tactile, honest.**

Three-word feel: **organized, credible, lived-in.**

The interface should evoke a well-kept workbench — a pegboard with every wrench in its place, a parts cabinet with hand-labeled drawers, a service log written in pencil. Grease-under-fingernails credibility. Not slick, not corporate, not aspirational-luxury. The user is a builder; the app should feel built _for_ builders by someone who actually rides.

**Emotional goals:** trust (this thing is reliable), pride (my collection looks good in here), ease (it stays out of my way), recognition (a fellow cyclist made this).

**Voice notes:** plain, specific, occasionally dry. "Crankset, 175mm, scratched bash ring" — not "Component item — see details for more."

### Aesthetic Direction

**Tactile / industrial — distinctly NOT generic Material 3.**

Even though React Native Paper (MD3) is the underlying component library, the surface layer should feel hand-set, not framework-default. We use MD3 for accessibility plumbing and theming hooks, then override its visual language wholesale.

**Visual cues to lean into:**

- Asymmetric, editorial layouts — varied scale, intentional whitespace, image-forward composition. Not identical card grids.
- Generous typographic moments — model years, tire widths, weights, gear ratios deserve to be **shown** as figures, not buried in body text.
- Field-journal / service-manual texture — hairline rules, paper-toned surfaces, the occasional rotated polaroid or stamped status mark.
- Photo-forward inventory — the user's own gear is the visual subject. Surfaces frame it; chrome doesn't compete with it.
- Honest UI — clear labels in plain language, function-revealing affordances, no decorative gradients or glow.

**Theme:** respect the user's appearance setting (system / light / dark). Design must work in both — workshop in daylight AND late-night build. Don't default-design for one and bolt on the other.

**Existing tokens (starting point, not sacred):**

- Primary teal `#006857` — strong brand anchor with workshop / aged-frame association. Keep as the brand soul.
  - Background `#f5f2ec` — warm paper/bone tone. ✅ Implemented.
  - Dark background `#141210` — warm charcoal. ✅ Implemented.
  - **Still open:** one warm accent for moments that need heat (a Park-Tool-blue counterpoint, an oxidized orange, a faded red) — picked once, used sparingly per the 60-30-10 rule. Not yet in the palette.
- Manrope font family is loaded (Regular → ExtraBold). Manrope is the **workhorse body / UI face** — keep it for titles, body, labels, and all UI controls.
- **Display font: Big Shoulders Display** (Black / ExtraBold / Bold) — a condensed industrial sans inspired by Chicago sign-painting. Heavy compressed forms at display/headline sizes evoke Shimano spec sheets, Park Tool packaging, and stamped part numbers. Spec figures in this font look like they belong on a chainring. **Do not** reach for serifs (Young Serif, Fraunces, Playfair, Cormorant) — they read as editorial/bookish, wrong for a technical parts inventory. **Do not** reach for sci-fi condensed faces (Rajdhani, Titillium, Exo) — they read as gaming, not workshop. **Do not** reach for Fraunces, Newsreader, Playfair Display, Cormorant, Inter, DM Serif, Plus Jakarta, or Instrument anything (training-data defaults).

### Design Principles

1. **Tactile, not flat.** Surfaces should feel like real material — paper, pegboard, anodized aluminum. Use subtle texture, hairline rules, and weighted typography. Reject MD3's neutral elevation aesthetic.

2. **Editorial, not templated.** Vary card sizes, break the grid intentionally for the featured part / recent build / status alert. Treat numbers as typography (year, mm, kg, °). No identical-rectangle inventory walls.

3. **Inventory-first hierarchy.** The photograph of the part and its identifying spec lead every screen. Status pills, distance chips, and social signals defer. The user already knows what an XT crankset looks like — don't over-label.

4. **Workshop honesty over polish.** Clear, specific labels. Plain language with the right vocabulary (cassette, not "rear gear thing"). No decorative gradients, no glassmorphism, no generic drop shadows. The aesthetic earns trust through clarity, not effects.

5. **Community-warm at the seams.** When the social layer surfaces — groups, lending, ratings, messaging — it should feel like a friend's nod (a warm accent, human phrasing) not a marketplace transaction (no "★ 4.8 (12)" by default).

### Hard Constraints

- **iOS + Android + Web parity.** Single codebase via Expo; design must render gracefully across all three. Container-aware layouts, touch-target minimums (44pt iOS / 48dp Android), keyboard / pointer support on web.
- **Outdoor / sunlight legibility.** Body text contrast ≥ WCAG AA in both themes; status colors readable in glare; primary actions large enough for gloved one-handed use.
- **React Native Paper / MD3 underlying.** Accessibility plumbing and theming hooks come from Paper — override visual tokens, don't fight the API.
- **Privacy-first ethos.** UI never surfaces precise location by default, never implies a stranger has access to private inventory, never reads as a marketplace-first product.

### Anti-references

- Generic Material 3 demos (Google's defaults).
- "Crypto-dashboard" aesthetic — neon cyan on near-black, purple-blue gradients, glowing accents.
- Marketplace card grids (Etsy / Depop / eBay-style identical product tiles).
- AI-slop tells: side-stripe accent borders on cards, gradient text, sparklines as decoration, big rounded icons over every heading, monospace-everywhere "technical" cosplay.
